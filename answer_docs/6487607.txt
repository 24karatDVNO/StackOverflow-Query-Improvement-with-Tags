<p>Take a look at Javascript closures.  It took me a while to get them but they will allow you to have private members.  <a href="http://www.crockford.com/javascript/private.html" rel="nofollow">Here's an article</a> by Douglas Crockford on them that's pretty straightforward, and <a href="https://developer.mozilla.org/en/Core_JavaScript_1.5_Guide/Working_with_Closures" rel="nofollow">another</a> by the Mozilla folks.</p>

<p>I use closures and expose selected methods through namespaces.  It works pretty well and helps us keep our code tidy.</p>
