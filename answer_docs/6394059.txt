<p>Macros don't respect scope, and a macro's name may not be available to a symbolic debugger. <a href="http://bit.ly/mLLT5g" rel="nofollow">Dan Saks</a> has a fairly complete article on the relative merits of macros (none), constant objects, and enumeration constants. Like <a href="http://semantics.org/cpp_gotchas/index.html" rel="nofollow">Stephen Dewhurst</a>, Saks prefers enumeration constants for integer values since they take up no storage (more precisely, enumeration constants have neither storage duration nor linkage).</p>
