<p>If the Objects you store in the List implement <code>equals</code> to match these attributes, then <a href="http://download.oracle.com/javase/1.4.2/docs/api/java/util/List.html#contains%28java.lang.Object%29" rel="nofollow">list.contains(o)</a> or <a href="http://download.oracle.com/javase/1.4.2/docs/api/java/util/List.html#indexOf%28java.lang.Object%29" rel="nofollow">list.indexOf(o)</a> will give you the answer.</p>

<p>If your objects do not implement <a href="http://download.oracle.com/javase/1.4.2/docs/api/java/lang/Object.html#equals%28java.lang.Object%29" rel="nofollow">equals</a>, then these methods will answer based on reference equality (not what you want).</p>
