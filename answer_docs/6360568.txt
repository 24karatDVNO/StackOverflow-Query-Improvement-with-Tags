<p>You can use a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base64#URL_applications" rel="nofollow">modified base 64 for URL Applications</a> which is just base64 with a couple of the problem characters replaced.</p>

<p>The easiest way is to take your base64 string and encode perform a string replace on the problem characters when building the URL, and reversing the process when interpreting the URL.</p>
