<p><code>Person</code> and <code>Tourist</code> implement <code>Identifiable&lt;String&gt;</code> and <code>Identifiable&lt;UTFString&gt;</code> - the same generic interface, but with different type parameters. The type parameters are <code>String</code> and <code>UTFString</code>. These two classes are both subclasses of <code>String</code> and superclasses of <code>UTFString</code>, so <em>both</em> <code>? extends String</code> and <code>? super UTFString</code> will be allowed. The difference between them is what you can do with <code>z</code>: with <code>? extends String</code>, you may not call methods that take the generic type as a parameter; with <code>? super UTFString</code>, you may not call methods that return the generic type. Since you call <code>z.setId()</code>, which seems to take the generic type as a parameter, this excludes <code>? extends String</code>.</p>
