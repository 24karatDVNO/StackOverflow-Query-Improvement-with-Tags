<p>If a suitable JRE isn't installed on the target machine, no Java executable wrapper is going to work. If a suitable JRE <em>is</em> on the machine, no executable wrapper that I know of would try to download a JRE.</p>

<p>There's a nice survey <a href="http://www.excelsior-usa.com/articles/java-to-exe.html" rel="nofollow">here</a> of alternative approaches, including ahead-of-time compilers (that turn your jar into native code), installers that can bundle a JRE, and other ideas.</p>
