<p>Depending on your latency and throughput requirements Xmpp may or may not be useful. If you were to write an internet distributed game, with social aspects and low content updates (say farmville) Xmpp would be useful, since it allows for easy scaling of many simulatenous users, tracking their online status, direct messaging between the users and the game components, etc.</p>

<p>For something fast paced, the overhead of the protocol likely makes unsuitable.</p>

<p>In the context of tcp/udp, xmpp is an xml stream protocol on top of tcp with centralized, but federated servers.</p>
